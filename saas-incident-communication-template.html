SaaS incident communication checklist

Use this before publishing an outage, degraded service, or incident update to customers. Keep private logs, customer data, credentials, internal channels, stack traces, and legal/security conclusions out of public tools.

1. State the customer-visible impact

  • Which service, component, feature, region, or customer group is affected?
  • What symptoms does the customer see?
  • What still works?
  • Is the incident investigating, identified, monitoring, resolved, scheduled maintenance, or post-incident?

2. Say what happens next

  • What mitigation or workaround is available?
  • Is customer action needed, or should customers wait?
  • Who owns the next customer update?
  • What exact time will the next update be published?

3. Keep channels consistent

  • Status page, email, in-app banner, and support macro should use the same impact and next-update time.
  • Support should know the escalation threshold.
  • Major incidents should have a follow-up or post-incident summary plan.

4. Avoid trust-risk wording

  • Avoid unsupported ETAs, fake certainty, vendor blame, panic language, or false reassurance.
  • Do not speculate about root cause, security, data loss, privacy, billing, or SLA impact.
  • Use only approved caveats when sensitive impact is implied.
